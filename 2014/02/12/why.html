Why?

12 Feb 2014

How often do you ask yourself: "Is it time for the new project?". Does the thought of it excites you? Have you caught yourself thinking about thrilling prototypes, projects and experiments you could be building and conviniently came up with a number of excuses why you won't do it just "right now". I have. Too many times. I have caught myself missing that "project excitement" outside of work and reading fantasy books instead, watching TV shows with my wife, messing around the house.

While I definitely continue reading fantasy books I do certainly miss the excitement of building games and game aspect prototypes. It is like a test to me, testing my ability to learn certain different elements, ability to connect them.

One of my biggest problems is the fact that once excitement ends and grind starts I often abandon project and move to the next thrilling challenge. While I cannot honestly claim to myself that I will be able to finish this project, having a pressure of documenting ongoing work and thought processes should be enough to pass grinding phrases.

Why strategy? There are too many aspects and components I haven't played with in strategies. I haven't done it before, dont really know where to start or what "proper" development entails. This is going to be fun.
